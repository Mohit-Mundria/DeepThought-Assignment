# Target Company Research Methodology
**Target Hub:** Pune Industrial Cluster (Chakan, Bhosari, Pirangut, Pimpri, Talegaon, Hadapsar, Mundhwa)  
**ICP Focus:** Basket C — Precision Auto Components & Engineering  

This document outlines the systematic, high-discipline data engineering and verification workflow utilized to identify, filter, and score 25 qualified target companies matching DeepThought’s "Federer" Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

---

## 1. Defining the Research Universe
To eliminate superficial Google search results, the target universe was defined around **Pune's precision engineering clusters**. The segment chosen (**Basket C — Precision Auto Components & Engineering**) provides an optimal landscape for discovering Federer companies due to specific industry dynamics:
* **High Operational Complexity:** Requires multi-axis CNC machines, synchronized tool rooms, and intense shop-floor coordination where DeepThought’s PDGMS operating system adds clear value.
* **Systems Familiarity:** These entities frequently engage with automotive Tier-1 or global export standards, forcing them to adopt basic ERPs and structured costing protocols early in their growth cycles.

---

## 2. Multi-Stage Sourcing Pipeline
The identification process bypassed broad keyword inquiries and executed a top-of-funnel capture across high-signal business registries:
---
[Stage 1: Registry Mining]
├── MCA Filings (Pune RoC) for U29/U34 industrial registration classifications
├── B2B Directories (Zauba Corp, Tofler, Tracxn, IndiaMart OEM lists)
└── Automated Extraction of companies within the ₹30Cr–₹500Cr revenue boundary
│
[Stage 2: Operational Gate Filtration]
├── Verification of active manufacturing sites (MIDC plots in Chakan, Bhosari, etc.)
└── Exclusion of pure traders, brokers, and testing labs (Eligibility Gate E1)
│
[Stage 3: Systems & Promoter Signal Checking]
├── Cross-referencing LinkedIn for IT/SAP personnel and job postings
└── Mapping leadership lineage via MCA director data to identify Gen-2 board entrants
│
[Stage 4: Granular Federer Scoring]
└── Evaluation against the 6 core criteria to isolate A & B-band prospects

## 3. Strict Gate Filtering Execution
Every candidate passed through two absolute eligibility checkpoints prior to undergoing final framework scoring:

### Gate E1: Producer Verification
* **Objective:** Ensure the entity owns physical production infrastructure and builds capability internally.
* **Method:** Analyzed the "Principal Business Activities" in MCA filings. Companies registered under trading or distribution codes were immediately eliminated. Websites were thoroughly screened to confirm the presence of dedicated plants, foundries, or tool rooms (e.g., confirming *Poojaa Precision Engg.* operates its own high-pressure die-casting foundry rather than sourcing blanks externally).

### Gate E2: Accessible Presence
* **Objective:** Ensure physical on-site access for DeepThought Operating Fellows within the Pune industrial belt.
* **Method:** Crossed-checked the factory location against the registered corporate office. Companies possessing registered offices in Pune but holding their primary operational facilities elsewhere (such as *Precision Auto Components*, whose manufacturing is entirely anchored in Nashik) were disqualified.

## 4. Analytical Application of the Federer Score
Scoring was based on cold, objective evidence. Companies were penalized heavily if they displayed structural gaps that hindered organizational maturity:

* **C3: Differentiation (Weight: 20):** Evaluated capability-based moats over basic commodity output. Points were awarded for advanced, non-commodity configurations—such as *Vanguard Aerospace* machining exotic titanium components, or *Dynamic Gear Technologies* hitting tight DIN Class 4 accuracy levels.
* **C4: Decision-Maker Quality (Weight: 15):** Looked for systems-driven operators. Founders who invested in formalized costing models or technical optimization pipelines scored highly, balancing out instances where a promoter lacked an IIT/PhD pedigree but demonstrated deep systems thinking.
* **C7: Systems Maturity (Weight: 20) - *The Structural Gate*:** This acted as the primary qualification filter. Companies operating via fragmented tools (paper or WhatsApp) received a score of 0 and were purged. Confirmed deployments of unified SAP, S/4HANA, or core MRP modules indicated an organization that had already institutionalized structured workflows.
* **C8: Leadership Succession (Weight: 15):** Verified long-term sustainability by evaluating active Gen-2 operational involvement via corporate appointment timelines, or the structural introduction of external professional executives (CFO/COO) to break up centralized founder control.

---

## 5. Key Discoveries and Core Lessons
1. **The Revenue Gap Trap:** Niche automotive component manufacturers often jump from ₹400Cr to over ₹600Cr rapidly when they secure a primary platform contract with a major domestic OEM like Tata Motors or Mahindra. Continual validation via recent monthly GST transaction data and corporate updates is vital to prevent wasting research time on companies that have grown too large for the target segment.
2. **Systems Over Academic Pedigree:** In the industrial components sector, excellent operator-founders frequently hold practical diplomas rather than elite university degrees. However, their systems maturity is often higher than that of academic founders, as evidenced by their willingness to deploy robust enterprise systems early to control scrap rates and tooling cycles. This confirms that systems maturity (C7) is the truest indicator of a company's readiness for operational consulting.
3. **The Private Equity Disqualifier:** Multiple fast-growing mid-market firms (e.g., *Sahney Kirkwood*) look like ideal prospects on paper but are actually majority-owned by institutional investment groups. These companies must be identified and removed early, as their operational choices are governed by institutional boards rather than an independent founder looking to build internal capabilities.
